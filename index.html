Filipino Cooking

Adobo

Ingredients:

  • Pork Shoulder or Pork Belly
  • Soy Sauce Or Fish Sauce
  • Black Peppercorns
  • Vinegar
  • Bay Leaves
  • True Love (Secret Ingredient)
  • Sugar
  • Garlic
  • Onions
  • Salt & Pepper
  • Calamansi

Instructions:

  • Prepare Meat: Cut pork into bite-sized pieces.
  • Marinate Pork: Mix pork with soy sauce/fish sauce, peppercorns, vinegar, and bay leaves. Marinate for 30 minutes or overnight.
  • Sauté Aromatics: Heat oil, sauté garlic until browned. Add onions, cook until soft.
  • Cook Pork: Add marinated pork with marinade. Brown pork on all sides.
  • Simmer Adobo: Add water to cover pork partially. Bring to a boil, then simmer for 40-50 minutes.
  • Season: Add salt, pepper, sugar, and calamansi to taste. Stir in the "true love" ingredient.
  • Reduce Sauce: Simmer uncovered if sauce is too thin.
  • Serve: Remove bay leaves and serve hot with steamed rice.

Sinigang na Baboy

Ingredients:

  • 1.5 lbs pork ribs or belly (cut into chunks)
  • 1 medium onion (quartered)
  • 2-3 tomatoes (quartered)
  • 4 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1 radish (labanos) (peeled and sliced)
  • 1-2 cups string beans (cut into 2-inch pieces)
  • 1-2 cups kangkong (water spinach) or bok choy
  • 1 packet tamarind soup base mix (or 1 cup fresh tamarind juice if available)
  • 4 cups water
  • 1-2 pork cubes (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fish sauce (optional, for additional flavor)

Instructions:

  • In a large pot, heat some oil over medium heat. Sauté garlic, onion, and tomatoes until the onions are translucent and the tomatoes are softened.
  • Add pork chunks to the pot and cook until browned.
  • Pour in 4 cups of water and add pork cubes if using. Bring to a boil, then lower the heat and let it simmer until the pork is tender, about 30-45 minutes.
  • Add radish and cook for about 5 minutes.
  • Add tamarind soup base mix (or tamarind juice) and stir well. Adjust seasoning with salt, pepper, and fish sauce if needed.
  • Add string beans and cook for an additional 5 minutes. Finally, add kangkong (or bok choy) and cook until just wilted.
  • Serve hot with steamed rice and enjoy!

Lechon

Ingredients:

  • Whole Pig
  • Lemon Grass
  • Garlic
  • Salt
  • Pepper
  • Bay Leaves
  • Cooking Oil

Instructions:

  • Brush with oil regularly during roasting.

Beef Tapa

Ingredients:

  • 500g beef sirloin or round steak (thinly sliced against the grain)
  • 4 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon oil(for frying)

Instructions:

  • Marinate the Beef: In a bowl, combine the minced garlic, soy sauce, vinegar, sugar, ground black pepper, and salt. Add the thinly sliced beef to the marinade and mix well. Ensure the beef is fully coated. Cover and refrigerate for at least 1 hour or overnight for better flavor.
  • Cook the Beef: Heat oil in a pan over medium heat. Remove the beef slices from the marinade and let excess marinade drip off. Fry the beef slices in the pan until they are browned and crispy on the edges, about 3-5 minutes per side. Do not overcrowd the pan; you may need to do this in batches. Once cooked, transfer the beef to a plate lined with paper towels to drain excess oil.
  • Serve: Server the beef tapa with steamed rice and optionally with a side of fried eggs. For a traditional Filipino touch, server it with garlic rice(sinangag) and a fried egg to make "tapsilog."

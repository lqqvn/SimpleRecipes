Filipino Cooking

Adobo

Ingredients:

  • Pork Shoulder or Pork Belly
  • Soy Sauce Or Fish Sauce
  • Black Peppercorns
  • Vinegar
  • Bay Leaves
  • True Love (Secret Ingredient)
  • Sugar
  • Garlic
  • Onions
  • Salt & Pepper
  • Calamansi Or Pineapple

Instructions:

  • Prepare Meat: Cut pork into bite-sized pieces.
  • Marinate Pork: Mix pork with soy sauce/fish sauce, peppercorns, vinegar, and bay leaves. Marinate for 30 minutes or overnight.
  • Sauté Aromatics: Heat oil, sauté garlic until browned. Add onions, cook until soft.
  • Cook Pork: Add marinated pork with marinade. Brown pork on all sides.
  • Simmer Adobo: Add water to cover pork partially. Bring to a boil, then simmer for 40-50 minutes.
  • Season: Add salt, pepper, sugar, and calamansi to taste. Stir in the "true love" ingredient.
  • Reduce Sauce: Simmer uncovered if sauce is too thin.
  • Serve: Remove bay leaves and serve hot with steamed rice.

Sinigang na Baboy

Ingredients:

  • 1.5 lbs pork ribs or belly (cut into chunks)
  • 1 medium onion (quartered)
  • 2-3 tomatoes (quartered)
  • 4 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1 radish (labanos) (peeled and sliced)
  • 1-2 cups string beans (cut into 2-inch pieces)
  • 1-2 cups kangkong (water spinach) or bok choy
  • 1 packet tamarind soup base mix (or 1 cup fresh tamarind juice if available)
  • 4 cups water
  • 1-2 pork cubes (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fish sauce (optional, for additional flavor)

Instructions:

  • In a large pot, heat some oil over medium heat. Sauté garlic, onion, and tomatoes until the onions are translucent and the tomatoes are softened.
  • Add pork chunks to the pot and cook until browned.
  • Pour in 4 cups of water and add pork cubes if using. Bring to a boil, then lower the heat and let it simmer until the pork is tender, about 30-45 minutes.
  • Add radish and cook for about 5 minutes.
  • Add tamarind soup base mix (or tamarind juice) and stir well. Adjust seasoning with salt, pepper, and fish sauce if needed.
  • Add string beans and cook for an additional 5 minutes. Finally, add kangkong (or bok choy) and cook until just wilted.
  • Serve hot with steamed rice and enjoy!

Beef Tapa

Ingredients:

  • 500g beef sirloin or round steak (thinly sliced against the grain)
  • 4 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon oil(for frying)

Instructions:

  • Marinate the Beef: In a bowl, combine the minced garlic, soy sauce, vinegar, sugar, ground black pepper, and salt. Add the thinly sliced beef to the marinade and mix well. Ensure the beef is fully coated. Cover and refrigerate for at least 1 hour or overnight for better flavor.
  • Cook the Beef: Heat oil in a pan over medium heat. Remove the beef slices from the marinade and let excess marinade drip off. Fry the beef slices in the pan until they are browned and crispy on the edges, about 3-5 minutes per side. Do not overcrowd the pan; you may need to do this in batches. Once cooked, transfer the beef to a plate lined with paper towels to drain excess oil.
  • Serve: Server the beef tapa with steamed rice and optionally with a side of fried eggs. For a traditional Filipino touch, server it with garlic rice(sinangag) and a fried egg to make "tapsilog."

Japanese Dish

Chicken Teriyaki

Ingredients:

  • 500g chicken thighs, boneless and skinless
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup mirin
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon ginger, grated
  • Green onions, chopped (for garnish)

Instructions:

  • In a bowl, mix soy sauce, mirin, sugar, garlic, and ginger to create the teriyaki sauce.
  • Heat sesame oil in a pan over medium heat.
  • Add chicken thighs and cook until browned on both sides.
  • Pour the teriyaki sauce over the chicken and simmer for 10-15 minutes, or until the chicken is cooked through and the sauce has thickened.
  • Garnish with chopped green onions and serve with rice.

Motsunabe

Ingredients:

  • 500g beef offal (such as beef tripe or intestine), cleaned and cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1 large onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon ginger, minced
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 4 cups beef broth or water
  • 3 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons miso paste (white or red)
  • 2 tablespoons sake (Japanese rice wine)
  • 1 tablespoon mirin (sweet rice wine)
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 200g tofu, cut into cubes
  • 1 cup mushrooms (shiitake, enoki, or button), sliced
  • 1 small cabbage, chopped
  • 2 green onions, chopped
  • Fresh coriander or parsley for garnish (optional)
  • Cooked rice or noodles for serving

Instructions:

  • Heat the vegetable oil in a large pot over medium heat.
  • Add the minced garlic and ginger, and cook until fragrant, about 1 minute.
  • Add the sliced onion and cook until softened and translucent, about 5 minutes.
  • Add the beef offal to the pot and cook until it starts to brown, about 5-7 minutes.
  • Pour in the beef broth or water, and bring to a simmer.
  • Stir in the soy sauce, miso paste, sake, mirin, sesame oil, and sugar. Mix well until the miso paste is fully dissolved.
  • Simmer the mixture for about 15-20 minutes, or until the offal is tender and cooked through.
  • Add the tofu, mushrooms, and chopped cabbage to the pot. Cook for an additional 5-10 minutes until the vegetables are tender.
  • Season with additional soy sauce or salt to taste, if needed.
  • Garnish with chopped green onions and fresh coriander or parsley, if desired.
  • Serve the Motsunabe hot with cooked rice or noodles.

Negima (Grilled Chicken and Leek Skewers)

Ingredients:

  • 500g chicken thigh, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1 bunch leeks, cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup mirin
  • 1/4 cup sake
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Wooden skewers, soaked in water

Instructions:

  • Mix soy sauce, mirin, sake, and sugar to create the marinade.
  • Marinate chicken pieces for at least 30 minutes.
  • Thread chicken and leeks onto skewers, alternating them.
  • Grill over medium heat, basting with marinade, until chicken is cooked through and slightly charred.
  • Season with salt and pepper before serving.

Yuba (Tofu Skin)

Ingredients:

  • 1 liter soy milk
  • 1/2 teaspoon coagulant (such as magnesium chloride or calcium sulfate)
  • Water (for mixing coagulant)
  • Optional: soy sauce, sugar for seasoning

Instructions:

  • Heat soy milk in a pot until it reaches 80°C (176°F).
  • Mix the coagulant with a small amount of water to create a solution.
  • Stir the coagulant solution into the hot soy milk and let it sit for 10-15 minutes to form curds.
  • Carefully remove the yuba skin that forms on the surface and let it drain on a towel or cooling rack.
  • Season with soy sauce and sugar if desired, or use as is in dishes.

Chawanmushi (Steamed Egg Custard)

Ingredients:

  • 4 large eggs
  • 2 cups dashi stock
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon mirin
  • 1/4 cup cooked shrimp, chopped
  • 1/4 cup sliced mushrooms
  • 1/4 cup cooked chicken, chopped
  • Green onions, chopped (for garnish)

Instructions:

  • Beat the eggs gently in a bowl.
  • Mix in dashi stock, soy sauce, and mirin.
  • Strain the mixture to remove any bubbles and impurities.
  • Place shrimp, mushrooms, and chicken into small heatproof cups or ramekins.
  • Pour the egg mixture over the ingredients in the cups.
  • Cover with foil and steam in a pot over simmering water for 15-20 minutes, or until the custard is set.
  • Garnish with green onions before serving.

Kakiage (Mixed Vegetable Tempura)

Ingredients:

  • 1 large carrot, julienned
  • 1 small zucchini, julienned
  • 1 small onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 cup tempura flour
  • 1 cup cold water
  • Oil for frying
  • Tempura dipping sauce (tsuyu)

Instructions:

  • Mix tempura flour with cold water until just combined; do not overmix.
  • Combine vegetables in a bowl.
  • Heat oil in a deep pan or pot to 180°C (350°F).
  • Dip vegetables in the batter and drop into hot oil in small spoonfuls, making sure not to overcrowd the pan.
  • Fry until golden brown and crispy, then drain on paper towels.
  • Serve with tempura dipping sauce.

Chirashi Sushi (Scattered Sushi)

  • 1 cucumber, sliced

  • Garnish with pickled ginger and wasabi as desired.

Shoyu Ramen

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups chicken or pork broth
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon miso paste
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, grated
  • 2 packs of ramen noodles
  • 2 green onions, sliced
  • 1 cup bamboo shoots, sliced
  • 1 cup mushrooms, sliced
  • 2 eggs, soft-boiled
  • 1/2 cup sliced cooked pork or chicken
  • Nori (seaweed) for garnish
  • Sesame seeds for garnish

Instructions:

  • In a large pot, heat a little oil over medium heat. Add garlic and ginger, and cook until fragrant.
  • Add the broth, soy sauce, and miso paste. Stir well and bring to a simmer.
  • Cook the ramen noodles according to package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  • Add bamboo shoots, mushrooms, and sliced pork or chicken to the broth. Simmer for about 5 minutes.
  • Divide the cooked noodles into bowls and pour the hot broth over them.
  • Top each bowl with soft-boiled eggs, green onions, nori, and sesame seeds.
  • Serve hot and enjoy!

Takoyaki (Octopus Balls)

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup takoyaki flour (or all-purpose flour with dashi powder)
  • 1 cup dashi stock
  • 1 egg
  • 100g cooked octopus, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons chopped green onions
  • 1/4 cup tempura scraps (tenkasu)
  • Takoyaki sauce
  • Kewpie mayonnaise
  • Bonito flakes (katsuobushi)
  • Seaweed flakes (aonori)
  • Oil for greasing the takoyaki pan

Instructions:

  • Mix takoyaki flour, dashi stock, and egg to make the batter.
  • Heat a takoyaki pan and lightly oil each cup.
  • Pour batter into each cup, filling about 3/4 full.
  • Add chopped octopus, green onions, and tempura scraps into each cup.
  • Cook for a few minutes, then use a skewer to turn the balls until they are evenly browned.
  • Serve with takoyaki sauce, mayonnaise, bonito flakes, and seaweed flakes.

Sushi Rolls

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups sushi rice
  • 2 1/2 cups water
  • 1/2 cup rice vinegar
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • Seaweed sheets (nori)
  • 1 cucumber, julienned

  • 200g fresh fish (like tuna or salmon), sliced (optional)

Instructions:

  • Rinse sushi rice under cold water until the water runs clear. Cook rice according to package instructions.

  • Roll the sushi tightly using the bamboo mat, then slice into pieces. Serve with soy sauce and wasabi.

Tempura

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup cornstarch
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1 egg
  • 1 cup ice-cold water
  • Vegetable oil, for frying
  • Assorted vegetables (e.g., sweet potatoes, bell peppers, zucchini), sliced
  • Shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • Tempura dipping sauce (tsuyu)

Instructions:

  • In a bowl, mix flour, cornstarch, and baking powder. Whisk together egg and cold water, then add to dry ingredients. Mix lightly; the batter should be lumpy.
  • Heat oil in a deep pan to 350°F (180°C).
  • Dip vegetables and shrimp into the batter and fry in batches until golden and crispy. Drain on paper towels.
  • Serve hot with tempura dipping sauce.

Okonomiyaki

Yakisoba

Ingredients:

  • 200g yakisoba noodles
  • 150g sliced pork or chicken
  • 1/2 cup sliced onion
  • 1 cup shredded cabbage
  • 1/2 cup sliced bell peppers (any color)
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 3 tbsp soy sauce
  • 2 tbsp Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 tbsp oyster sauce (optional)
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Chopped green onions for garnish
  • Shredded nori (seaweed) for garnish (optional)

Instructions:

  • Cook the yakisoba noodles according to the package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  • In a large pan or wok, heat the vegetable oil over medium-high heat.
  • Add the sliced pork or chicken and cook until browned and cooked through.
  • Add the minced garlic and sliced onions to the pan and cook until the onions are translucent.
  • Add the shredded cabbage and bell peppers to the pan and stir-fry until they begin to soften.
  • Add the cooked noodles to the pan and toss to combine with the vegetables and meat.
  • In a small bowl, mix together the soy sauce, Worcestershire sauce, oyster sauce (if using), and sugar.
  • Pour the sauce mixture over the noodles and stir well to ensure everything is evenly coated.
  • Season with salt and pepper to taste.

Osso Buco

Ingredients:

  • 4 veal shanks (about 1.5-2 inches thick)
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 carrots, diced
  • 2 celery stalks, diced
  • 1 cup white wine
  • 1 can (14.5 oz) crushed tomatoes
  • 2 cups beef broth
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 tsp dried thyme
  • 1 lemon, zested
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  • Preheat oven to 325°F (163°C).
  • Dredge veal shanks in flour, shaking off excess.
  • Heat olive oil in a large ovenproof pot over medium-high heat. Brown veal shanks on all sides, then remove and set aside.
  • In the same pot, add onion, garlic, carrots, and celery. Cook until softened.
  • Add white wine, scraping up any browned bits from the bottom of the pot. Stir in crushed tomatoes, beef broth, bay leaf, and thyme.
  • Return veal shanks to the pot. Bring to a simmer.
  • Cover and transfer to the oven. Cook for 2-2.5 hours, or until the meat is tender and falling off the bone.
  • Garnish with lemon zest and chopped parsley before serving.

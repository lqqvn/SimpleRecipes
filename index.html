Pork And Chicken Adobo

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 lbs pork belly, chopped
  • 1 1/2 lbs chicken, cut into serving pieces
  • 4 pieces dried bay leaves
  • 2 teaspoons whole peppercorn
  • 1 head garlic, slightly crushed
  • 6 tablespoons white vinegar
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce
  • 2 teaspoons brown sugar
  • 2 cups water
  • Salt to taste
  • 3 tablespoons cooking oil

Instructions:

  1. Heat the oil in a pan.
  2. Once the oil becomes hot, add the garlic. Cook until the color turns golden brown.
  3. Remove the garlic and set aside. Add pork and chicken. Cook for 5 minutes or until the color turns light brown.
  4. Add whole peppercorn, bay leaves, oyster sauce, soy sauce, and water. Let boil and simmer until the meat gets tender.
  5. Add the sugar and stir.
  6. Pour-in vinegar and let boil.Simmer until most of the liquid evaporates.
  7. Add salt to taste. Put-in the fried garlic, stir, and cook for 2 minutes.
  8. Serve. Share and enjoy!

Sinigang na Baboy

Ingredients:

  • 1.5 lbs pork ribs or belly (cut into chunks)
  • 1 medium onion (quartered)
  • 2-3 tomatoes (quartered)
  • 4 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1 radish (labanos) (peeled and sliced)
  • 1-2 cups string beans (cut into 2-inch pieces)
  • 1-2 cups kangkong (water spinach) or bok choy
  • 1 packet tamarind soup base mix (or 1 cup fresh tamarind juice if available)
  • 4 cups water
  • 1-2 pork cubes (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fish sauce (optional, for additional flavor)

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, heat some oil over medium heat. Sauté garlic, onion, and tomatoes until the onions are translucent and the tomatoes are softened.
  2. Add pork chunks to the pot and cook until browned.
  3. Pour in 4 cups of water and add pork cubes if using. Bring to a boil, then lower the heat and let it simmer until the pork is tender, about 30-45 minutes.
  4. Add radish and cook for about 5 minutes.
  5. Add tamarind soup base mix (or tamarind juice) and stir well. Adjust seasoning with salt, pepper, and fish sauce if needed.
  6. Add string beans and cook for an additional 5 minutes. Finally, add kangkong (or bok choy) and cook until just wilted.
  7. Serve hot with steamed rice and enjoy!

Laing

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups of coconut milk(add 1 cup water if using coconut cream)
  • 1 medium onion (chopped)
  • 2 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 8-10 pieces dried fish or smoked Fish
  • 5 cups dried Taro leaves
  • 3 pieces labuyo chili or 5 pieces bird's eye chili - each cut into 4-5 pieces

Instructions:

  1. Place coconut milk, water, onion, garlic and dried fish in a pot. Bring to boil over medium-low heat.
  2. Once it starts to boil, add the dried taro leaves. Cover pot with lid and let it boil for 10 minutes without stirring.
  3. Add the chilis. Season with salt if still needed.
  4. Let it boil until coconut milk is reduced or almost completely gone.

Pinakbet

Ingredients:

  • 1 tablespoon oil
  • 1/2 pound pork belly (cut into strips)
  • 1 small onion (chopped)
  • 2 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 2-3 tablespoons shrimp paste
  • 3 medium tomatoes (chopped)
  • 1/2 small squash (peeled and cut into cubes)
  • 1 cup of water
  • 5-7 young okras (ends trimmed, cut into halves diagonally)
  • 1/2 bunch yard-long beans (sitaw) - ends trimmed and cut into 3-inch lengths
  • 1 medium bitter gourd - seeded then halved and cut into slices
  • 1 large eggplant - ends trimmed and cut into cubes
  • Salt

Instructions:

  1. In a wok or pan, heat oil over medium-high heat. Add pork and cook until browned and fat rendered.

  2. Lower heat to medium and add the onion and garlic and cook until tender and aromatic. Add tomatoes and cook for about 3-5 minutes or until it becomes soft and the skins peel off the flesh
  3. Add shrimp paste and continue to cook for about a minute.
  4. Add squash and water. Cover the pan and let it simmer for about 2 minutes
  5. Add yard-long beans, bitter gourd, and eggplant. Season with salt if needed. Cook for about 2 minutes. Add the okra and cook for another 2 minutes or until vegetables are tender yet still a bit crisp
  6. Transfer to a serving plate and serve with rice and your favorite fried meat.

Tortang Talong

Ingredients:

  • 4 Pieces of Chinese eggplant or a normal eggplant
  • 2 pieces of raw egg
  • 1 teaspoon of salt
  • 6 tablespoons of cooking oil

Instructions:

  1. Grill the eggplant until the color of skin turns almost black
  2. Let the eggplant cool for a while then peel off the skin. Set aside.
  3. Crack the eggs and place in a bowl.
  4. Add salt and beat
  5. Place the eggplant on a flat surface and flatten using a fork.
  6. Dip the flattened eggplant in the beaten egg mixture
  7. Heat the pan and pour the cooking oil
  8. Fry the eggplant (that was dipped in the beaten mixture). Makes sure that both sides are cooked. Frying time will take you about 3 to 4 minutes per side on medium heat.

Bistek Tagalog

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 lbs beef sirloin (thinly sliced)
  • 5 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 4 pieces calamansi or (1 piece of lemon)
  • 1/2 tsp ground black pepper
  • 3 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 3 pieces onion (sliced into rings)
  • 4 tablespoons of cooking oil
  • 1 cup of water
  • 1 pinch salt

Instructions:

  1. Marinate beef in soy sauce, lemon (or calamansi), and ground black pepper for at least 1 hour. Note: marinate overnight for best result (1 1/2 lbs beef sirloin, 5 tablespoons soy sauce, 4 pieces of calamansi, 1/2 tsp ground black pepper)
  2. Heat the cooking oil in a pan then pan-fry half of the onions until the texture becomes soft. Set aside (4 tablespoons cooking oil, 3 pieces onion)
  3. Drain the marinade from the beef. Set it aside. Pan-fry the beef on the same pan where the onions were fried for 1 minute per side. Remove from the pan. Set aside (1 1/2 lbs beef sirloin)
  4. Add more oil if needed. Saute garlic and remaining raw onions until onion softens. (3 cloves garlic)
  5. Pour the remaining marinade and water. Bring to a boil. (1 cup water)
  6. Add beef. Cover the pan and simmer until meat is tender. Note: Add water as needed
  7. Season with ground black pepper and salt as needed. Top with pan-fried onions (1 pinch salt)
  8. And lastly transfer to a serving plate. SErve hot. Share and Enjoy!

Chopsuey

Ingredients:

  • 3-4 boneless skinless chicken breasts (thinly sliced)
  • 1/2 tsp baking soda
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 2 tbsp garlic (minced)

  • 3 stems bok choy

Chop Suey Sauce

  • 1 tbsp cornstarch
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 2 1/2 tbsp oyster sauce
  • 1 tbsp shaoxing wine
  • 1 tsp seasme oil
  • 1 tsp white pepper
  • 1 cut water

Instructions:

  1. First, tenderize or “velvet” the chicken. Coat the chicken evenly in the baking soda and set aside for about 20-30 minutes. While the chicken sits, make the sauce.
  2. To make chop suey sauce, start by whisking the cornstarch and soy sauce in bowl and then add in the oyster sauce, shaoxing wine, sesame oil, white, pepper, and water. Once the sauce is mixed, heat the vegetable oil on high heat in a wok. Add the garlic and onion and cook for about a minute, then add the chicken and cook for another minute. Once the chicken changes from pink to white, add in your veggies except for the bean sprouts and stir fry for an additional minute. Add in the bean sprouts and sauce. Stir fry until the sauce thickens and the vegetables remain crisp. Serve immediately over rice.

Pancit Malabon

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb thick rice noodles
  • 1/2 lb pork belly (boiled and sliced)
  • 1/2 cup annatto seeds diluted in 1/2 cup water (also known as annatto water)
  • 3/4 cup shrimp juice
  • 3 tablespoons fish sauce (patis)
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 1 tablespoon fresh garlic (minced)
  • 1 medium-sized onion (minced)
  • 1/4 cup pork rinds (chicharon, pounded)
  • 1/2 lb shrimp (shelled, cooked, and halved lengthwise)
  • 1/4 lb adobong pusit (sliced)
  • 1/2 cup pork rinds (chicharon, pounded)
  • 1 cup napa cabbage (pechay baguio, chopped then blanched)
  • 1 piece lemon (quartered)
  • 3/4 cup tinapa flakes
  • 3 pieces hard boiled eggs (sliced)
  • 1 tablespoon parsley (chopped)
  • 2 tablespoons toasted garlic

Instructions:

  1. Cook the noodles according to package instructions. If you are using the traditional Pancit Malabon noodles, you will need to soak it in water overnight before cooking. Set aside.
  2. Start making the sauce by sautéing the minced garlic and onion.
  3. Add chopped boiled pork and cook for 3 minutes.
  4. Add fish sauce and ground black pepper then stir.
  5. Pour-in shrimp juice and annatto water and let boil.
  6. Add pounded pork rinds (chicharon) then stir.
  7. Simmer for 2 to 3 minutes then turn off heat.
  8. Place the cooked noodles in a large bowl then pour-in the sauce and mix well.
  9. Transfer the sauce and noodle mixture in a wide serving plate and arrange the tinapa flakes, eggs, parsley, garlic, and cabbage on top.
  10. Serve with lemon or calamansi and a lot of love. Share and enjoy!

Ginisang Munggo

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups Mung beans
  • 1 tbsp garlic
  • 1/2 lb pork (thinly sliced)
  • 2 cups spinach (or alugbati)
  • 1 piece tomato (chopped)
  • 1 piece onion (chopped)
  • 9 pieces shrimp (optional)
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 24 ounces water (for boiling)
  • 1 piece knorr beef cube (for falvoring)
  • 1/2 cup crushed pork rind (chicharon)
  • 1.4 teaspoon ground black pepper

Instructions:

  1. In a pan, put-in the water and bring to a boil
  2. Put-in the Mung beans and simmer until becomes soft (about 35 to 50 minutes)
  3. On a separate pan, sauté the garlic,onion, and tomato
  4. Add the pork. Cook for 5 mins
  5. Put-in the beef cube and fish sauce. Simmer for 10 mins or until the meat is tender. Note: If necessary, you may add water to help make the meat tender but make sure to add more time to simmer

Bagoong Fried Rice

Ingredients:

  • Rice: Best to use day-old or leftover long-grain white rice like Jasmine or Basmati. Freshly cooked rice should be cooled and dried slightly.
  • Chinese Sausage: A dried, hard sausage with a sweet and savory flavor. Can be substituted with proteins like Spam, tocino, tapa, chorizo, or other sausages, and leftover cooked meats.
  • Sautéed Shrimp Paste (Ginisang Bagoong Alamang): Made from small shrimp or krill, salted, fermented, and sautéed with aromatics and seasonings. Use sparingly and adjust to taste.

Instructions:

  1. Fluff The Rice
  2. Brown the sausage
  3. Sauté the aromatics
  4. Add the shrimp paste (PRO TIP: Start with a small spoonful of shrimp paste, and add more as needed. Its flavor is quite intense, so you won't need much)
  5. Add the rice and sausage (toss in the rice and sausage pieces, mixing them into the sautéed ingredients at the bottom of the pan to combine everything well.)
  6. Combine and season (Cook until everything is thoroughly heated through and well combined. Season with salt and ground pepper to taste, then turn off the heat.)
  7. Serve your Bagoong Fried Rice with optional garnishes like scrambled eggs cut into thin strips, cilantro, cucumber, tomatoes, or green mango slices.

Beef Tapa

Ingredients:

  • Beef: Uses tender sirloin, requiring only a few minutes of pan frying. For tougher cuts like bottom round, add 1 cup of water and simmer until tender before finishing in oil.
  • Calamansi Juice: Adds tanginess and tenderizes meat. Can substitute with lemon, lime, pickle juice, vinegar, or wine.
  • Soy Sauce: Acts as a brining agent and enhances savory flavors.
  • Fresh Minced Garlic: Adds flavor depth; fresh is best, but garlic powder is a suitable alternative.
  • Sugar: Sweetens the marinade, balances acidity, and aids in caramelization.
  • Salt: Seasons and acts as a brining agent.
  • Pepper: Adds a kick of heat.

Instructions:

  1. Combine soy sauce, calamansi juice, garlic, sugar, salt, and pepper in a non-reactive bowl. Stir until sugar and salt are dissolved.
  2. Add beef and massage to coat it fully with marinade. Refrigerate overnight to cure. Drain the meat from the marinade.
  3. Heat oil in a wide pan. Add beef in a single layer and cook until liquid expelled from the meat is almost absorbed.
  4. Add more oil and cook until the meat is lightly browned and caramelized.
  5. Remove from pan and cut into serving portions. Serve hot.

Chicken Teriyaki

Ingredients:

  • 500g chicken thighs, boneless and skinless
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup mirin
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon ginger, grated
  • Green onions, chopped (for garnish)

Instructions:

  • In a bowl, mix soy sauce, mirin, sugar, garlic, and ginger to create the teriyaki sauce.
  • Heat sesame oil in a pan over medium heat.
  • Add chicken thighs and cook until browned on both sides.
  • Pour the teriyaki sauce over the chicken and simmer for 10-15 minutes, or until the chicken is cooked through and the sauce has thickened.
  • Garnish with chopped green onions and serve with rice.

Motsunabe

Ingredients:

  • 500g beef offal (such as beef tripe or intestine), cleaned and cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1 large onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon ginger, minced
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 4 cups beef broth or water
  • 3 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons miso paste (white or red)
  • 2 tablespoons sake (Japanese rice wine)
  • 1 tablespoon mirin (sweet rice wine)
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 200g tofu, cut into cubes
  • 1 cup mushrooms (shiitake, enoki, or button), sliced
  • 1 small cabbage, chopped
  • 2 green onions, chopped
  • Fresh coriander or parsley for garnish (optional)
  • Cooked rice or noodles for serving

Instructions:

  • Heat the vegetable oil in a large pot over medium heat.
  • Add the minced garlic and ginger, and cook until fragrant, about 1 minute.
  • Add the sliced onion and cook until softened and translucent, about 5 minutes.
  • Add the beef offal to the pot and cook until it starts to brown, about 5-7 minutes.
  • Pour in the beef broth or water, and bring to a simmer.
  • Stir in the soy sauce, miso paste, sake, mirin, sesame oil, and sugar. Mix well until the miso paste is fully dissolved.
  • Simmer the mixture for about 15-20 minutes, or until the offal is tender and cooked through.
  • Add the tofu, mushrooms, and chopped cabbage to the pot. Cook for an additional 5-10 minutes until the vegetables are tender.
  • Season with additional soy sauce or salt to taste, if needed.
  • Garnish with chopped green onions and fresh coriander or parsley, if desired.
  • Serve the Motsunabe hot with cooked rice or noodles.

Negima (Grilled Chicken and Leek Skewers)

Ingredients:

  • 500g chicken thigh, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1 bunch leeks, cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup mirin
  • 1/4 cup sake
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Wooden skewers, soaked in water

Instructions:

  • Mix soy sauce, mirin, sake, and sugar to create the marinade.
  • Marinate chicken pieces for at least 30 minutes.
  • Thread chicken and leeks onto skewers, alternating them.
  • Grill over medium heat, basting with marinade, until chicken is cooked through and slightly charred.
  • Season with salt and pepper before serving.

Yuba (Tofu Skin)

Ingredients:

  • 1 liter soy milk
  • 1/2 teaspoon coagulant (such as magnesium chloride or calcium sulfate)
  • Water (for mixing coagulant)
  • Optional: soy sauce, sugar for seasoning

Instructions:

  • Heat soy milk in a pot until it reaches 80°C (176°F).
  • Mix the coagulant with a small amount of water to create a solution.
  • Stir the coagulant solution into the hot soy milk and let it sit for 10-15 minutes to form curds.
  • Carefully remove the yuba skin that forms on the surface and let it drain on a towel or cooling rack.
  • Season with soy sauce and sugar if desired, or use as is in dishes.

Chawanmushi (Steamed Egg Custard)

Ingredients:

  • 4 large eggs
  • 2 cups dashi stock
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon mirin
  • 1/4 cup cooked shrimp, chopped
  • 1/4 cup sliced mushrooms
  • 1/4 cup cooked chicken, chopped
  • Green onions, chopped (for garnish)

Instructions:

  • Beat the eggs gently in a bowl.
  • Mix in dashi stock, soy sauce, and mirin.
  • Strain the mixture to remove any bubbles and impurities.
  • Place shrimp, mushrooms, and chicken into small heatproof cups or ramekins.
  • Pour the egg mixture over the ingredients in the cups.
  • Cover with foil and steam in a pot over simmering water for 15-20 minutes, or until the custard is set.
  • Garnish with green onions before serving.

Kakiage (Mixed Vegetable Tempura)

Ingredients:

  • 1 large carrot, julienned
  • 1 small zucchini, julienned
  • 1 small onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 cup tempura flour
  • 1 cup cold water
  • Oil for frying
  • Tempura dipping sauce (tsuyu)

Instructions:

  • Mix tempura flour with cold water until just combined; do not overmix.
  • Combine vegetables in a bowl.
  • Heat oil in a deep pan or pot to 180°C (350°F).
  • Dip vegetables in the batter and drop into hot oil in small spoonfuls, making sure not to overcrowd the pan.
  • Fry until golden brown and crispy, then drain on paper towels.
  • Serve with tempura dipping sauce.

Oyakodon (Chicken and Egg Rice Bowl)

Ingredients:

  • 2 chicken thighs, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 cup dashi stock
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon mirin
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • Cooked rice (for serving)
  • Chopped green onions (for garnish)

Instructions:

  • In a pan, combine dashi stock, soy sauce, mirin, and sugar. Bring to a simmer.

Chirashi Sushi (Scattered Sushi)

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups sushi rice, cooked
  • 1/4 cup rice vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 1 tablespoon salt
  • Assorted sashimi (salmon, tuna, etc.), sliced
  • 1 cucumber, sliced
  • 1 avocado, sliced
  • Pickled ginger (for garnish)
  • Wasabi (for garnish)

Instructions:

  • Mix rice vinegar, sugar, and salt in a bowl until dissolved.
  • Gently fold the vinegar mixture into the cooked sushi rice while it is still warm.
  • Allow the rice to cool to room temperature.
  • Spread the sushi rice in a large serving dish.
  • Top with assorted sashimi, cucumber slices, and avocado slices.
  • Garnish with pickled ginger and wasabi as desired.

Shoyu Ramen

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups chicken or pork broth
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon miso paste
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, grated
  • 2 packs of ramen noodles
  • 2 green onions, sliced
  • 1 cup bamboo shoots, sliced
  • 1 cup mushrooms, sliced
  • 2 eggs, soft-boiled
  • 1/2 cup sliced cooked pork or chicken
  • Nori (seaweed) for garnish
  • Sesame seeds for garnish

Instructions:

  • In a large pot, heat a little oil over medium heat. Add garlic and ginger, and cook until fragrant.
  • Add the broth, soy sauce, and miso paste. Stir well and bring to a simmer.
  • Cook the ramen noodles according to package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  • Add bamboo shoots, mushrooms, and sliced pork or chicken to the broth. Simmer for about 5 minutes.
  • Divide the cooked noodles into bowls and pour the hot broth over them.
  • Top each bowl with soft-boiled eggs, green onions, nori, and sesame seeds.
  • Serve hot and enjoy!

Osso Buco

Ingredients:

  • 4 veal shanks (about 1.5-2 inches thick)
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 carrots, diced
  • 2 celery stalks, diced
  • 1 cup white wine
  • 1 can (14.5 oz) crushed tomatoes
  • 2 cups beef broth
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 tsp dried thyme
  • 1 lemon, zested
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  • Preheat oven to 325°F (163°C).
  • Dredge veal shanks in flour, shaking off excess.
  • Heat olive oil in a large ovenproof pot over medium-high heat. Brown veal shanks on all sides, then remove and set aside.
  • In the same pot, add onion, garlic, carrots, and celery. Cook until softened.
  • Add white wine, scraping up any browned bits from the bottom of the pot. Stir in crushed tomatoes, beef broth, bay leaf, and thyme.
  • Return veal shanks to the pot. Bring to a simmer.
  • Cover and transfer to the oven. Cook for 2-2.5 hours, or until the meat is tender and falling off the bone.
  • Garnish with lemon zest and chopped parsley before serving.
